Description

"The God of Small Things" is a novel by Indian author Arundhati Roy, first published in 1997. The book tells the story of a family in the southern Indian state of Kerala and the tragic events that befall them. The story is narrated from the perspective of two young twins, Estha and Rahel, and switches back and forth between different time periods. The novel explores themes such as love, loss, family, caste, politics, and social injustice. "The God of Small Things" is known for its poetic language and smells of Kerala.
